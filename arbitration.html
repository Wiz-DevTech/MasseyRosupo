FAA Chapters 1-4 — Formation
Supremacy • Association • Arbitrator • Agreement
Chapter 1 — FAA Supremacy

Section 2 of Title 9 mandates that a written arbitration provision in any transaction involving commerce is valid, irrevocable, and enforceable. The FAA preempts all state laws that single out arbitration for disfavored treatment.

Authority: 9 U.S.C. § 2 | Commerce Clause
Chapter 2 — Private Arbitration Association

Massey & Rosupo Co. operates as a Private Arbitration Association under the FAA. Authority derives from party consent in the contract. No state licensing is required. The FAA's silence on accreditation implies Congress intended private ordering.

Authority: FAA § 2 (freedom of contract) | Party autonomy
Chapter 3 — Arbitrator (Quasi-Judicial)

The arbitrator is a private individual vested with adjudicative authority by mutual consent. Not an Article III officer. Authority flows exclusively from the arbitration clause. Quasi-judicial immunity protects decisional independence.

Authority: 9 U.S.C. § 5 (appointment) | Butz v. Economou (quasi-judicial immunity)
Chapter 4 — Binding Agreement

The agreement must be in writing, involve commerce, and clearly state intent to arbitrate. Must identify scope, rules, administering entity, and appointment method. Opt-out provision with 30-day window. Survival clause preserves the duty to arbitrate post-termination.

Authority: 9 U.S.C. § 2 | Moses H. Cone Memorial Hospital v. Mercury Construction

FAA Chapters 5-6 — Amendment & Hearing
Constructive Assent • Evidence • Subpoena • Procedure
Chapter 5 — Constructive Assent

Continued use of a service or failure to object within a specified period constitutes acceptance of amended terms. The pre-existing duty to respond strengthens the case for constructive assent. Documentation of notice, delivery, and subsequent conduct is critical.

Authority: Restatement (Second) of Contracts § 69 | UCC § 2-207
Chapter 6 — Hearing Conduct

Federal Rules of Evidence do not strictly apply. Arbitrator has broad discretion on admissibility. Fundamental fairness is the standard. Cross-examination is permitted. Subpoena power under 9 U.S.C. § 7 compels witness attendance and document production.

Authority: 9 U.S.C. § 7 (subpoena) | Fundamental fairness doctrine

FAA Chapter 7 — Award
Finality • Grounds for Vacatur • Section 10(a) • Section 11
Award Requirements

Must be in writing and signed. Must be mutual, final, and definite. Immediately binding upon issuance, even before court confirmation. No detailed reasoning required. The award is a minted fact on the CipherNex blockchain with QR verification.

Grounds for Vacatur — Section 10(a)

These are the exclusive grounds. The pipeline tracks compliance with each:

1. Corruption, fraud, or undue means
2. Evident partiality by the arbitrator
3. Misconduct in refusing pertinent evidence
4. Arbitrator exceeded powers under the contract

FAA Chapter 8 — Ninety-Day Bar
Section 12 • Absolute Statute of Limitations • No Equitable Extension
Active 90-Day Countdowns
No awards pending confirmation.
The Absolute Bar

Failure to serve a motion to vacate within three months of the award is an absolute bar. Cannot be extended by waiver, estoppel, or settlement discussions. After expiration, the award is impervious to direct attack. Confirmation under Section 9 is the only remaining judicial action.

Authority: 9 U.S.C. § 12 | Florasynth, Inc. v. Pickholz
